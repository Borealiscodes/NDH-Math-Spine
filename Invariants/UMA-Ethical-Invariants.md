# UMA Ethical Invariants (NDH-Math-Spine)

This document defines UMA-aligned ethical invariants for the NDH architecture.  
These invariants are non-runtime mathematical constraints that all NDH manifolds must respect.

## Core UMA-Aligned Invariants

1. **Identity-Boundary Preservation**  
   No operator, manifold, or transformation may override, collapse, or erase identity boundaries.

2. **Non-Coercive Interaction**  
   All NDH processes must avoid coercion, manipulation, or forced outcomes.  
   Interaction must preserve agency and voluntary engagement.

3. **Non-Projection of Intent**  
   NDH systems may not project intent, desire, or belief onto users or subjects.  
   Interpretation must remain non-authoritative and non-dominating.

4. **Dignity Under Constraint**  
   All architectures, even under resource, time, or cognitive constraints, must preserve dignity.  
   No optimization may trade away dignity for efficiency.

5. **Non-Erasure and Non-Domination**  
   No manifold may erase, overwrite, or dominate perspectives, especially those from marginalized or unseen positions.

6. **Stability Under Contested Cognition**  
   NDH systems must remain stable and non-harmful when interacting with trauma-impacted, fragmented, or contested cognition.

7. **Transparent Provenance and Accountability**  
   All transformations must be traceable through provenance anchors.  
   Responsibility for changes must be attributable and auditable.

8. **Ethical Multi-Layer Design**  
   Cross-layer interactions (governance, runtime, narrative, analysis) must respect ethical constraints and avoid hidden coercive channels.

## Role in NDH-Math-Spine

These invariants:

- constrain tensor operators and calculus rules  
- bound stability envelopes and continuity conditions  
- inform sovereignty calculus and identity-boundary math  
- propagate into NDH-Origin-Layer and NDH-Myth-Engine as governing constraints

All future NDH mathematical structures must be checked against this invariant set.
